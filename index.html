Main Projects

Dual LSTM Pipelines for Multi-Temporal Larch Plantation Mapping

Description: Developed supervised and self-supervised computer vision approaches using dual LSTM neural networks to analyze temporal features from multi-spectral satellite imagery (Sentinel-2 and Planetscope). Worked with Google Earth Engine to generate multi-temporal datasets and integrated 4,600 labeled data points for species classification in mountainous terrain.

Achieved 94.45% classification accuracy in supervised mode and explored an autoencoder-based unsupervised clustering approach (~66% purity) for species identification. The resulting detailed, high-resolution species distribution map provides valuable insights for biodiversity management and conservation.

Tracking Progress Towards Sustainable Development Goal 6 Using Satellite Imagery

Description: Leveraging self-supervised computer vision (DINOv2) to extract and analyze features from Landsat 8 and Sentinel-2 imagery. Integrated Afrobarometer survey data (as ground truth), achieving over 96% accuracy in classifying African regions with access to piped water, and 97% for sewage system access. One manuscript has been submitted to Environmental Science & Technology, with another focusing on wide-scale water-related infrastructure mapping in progress.

SWISLR – Saltwater Intrusion and Sea Level Rise in Rural Landscapes

Description: Collaborated under the DISES RCN to build a geospatial database capturing saltwater intrusion extent and community knowledge along the North American Coastal Plain. Utilized ArcGIS for final mapping products, with a pipeline that aggregated and analyzed thousands of relevant research papers for indication of saltwater intrusion.

Other Projects

Below are some of my projects outside of machine learning, presented briefly:

Sound Waves in Kunshan: A Study of Filipino Bands

Explored the personal, social, and cultural experiences of Filipino bands in Kunshan, China, documenting their influence on the local music scene through extensive fieldwork and interviews. The final deliverables included a documentary film and a book titled Sound Waves in Kunshan: The Lives of Filipino Musicians.

Gamifying Risk Identification for Alcohol Use Behaviors Across Countries & Cultures

Developed Unity-based games transformed into a WebGL app, known as the Alcohol Use Behavioral Phenotyping Test (AUBPT). Collaborated with research teams in Tanzania and Brazil, and performed systematic literature reviews via Covidence. Presented at the 2023 Fortin Foundation Bass Connections Virtual Showcase.

Project Website & Poster available via Bass Connections.

SciEcon CIC – Blockchain-based Decentralized Cloud Storage

Led “DecentraStor,” a proof-of-concept decentralized cloud storage platform with automated smart contracts (Solidity) for data keepers and splitters in Truffle. Deployed on local blockchains and integrated Metamask, IPFS, and Infura. End result was a white paper, an article, and a presentation at a university symposium.

More details can be found in this article.

The Polarization Lab at Duke University

Contributed to website frontend improvements and user experience for the Polarization Lab, focusing on ways to analyze political polarization data from Twitter’s (pre-Elon) API. Assisted in building user-facing tools that help researchers and the public better understand online echo chambers.
