Dual LSTM Pipelines for Multi-Temporal Larch Plantation Mapping

Fall 2024, Duke Kunshan University

Developed supervised and self-supervised LSTM models to classify tree species (Japanese and Chinese larch) from satellite imagery in mountainous terrains.
Keywords: Remote Sensing, LSTM, Google Earth Engine, Biodiversity Conservation

  • Accuracy: 94.45% supervised, ~66% unsupervised purity
  • Manuscript: Download PDF
  • Poster: Changqing Region Plantation Mapping Poster
  • Status: Submitted to Remote Sensing of Environment / Forest Ecology and Management journals

Tracking Progress Towards Sustainable Development Goal 6 Using Satellite Imagery

Used self-supervised vision model DINOv2 with Landsat-8 and Sentinel-2 data to classify infrastructure related to water access in Africa.

  • Accuracy: 96% (Piped Water), 97% (Sewage System)
  • Manuscript #1: Environmental Science and Technology Submission
  • Manuscript #2: Under preparation (Infrastructure Mapping)
